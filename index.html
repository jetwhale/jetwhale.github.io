Was Superbowl 52 an outlier or has the NFL become more offensive?

Sahil Mariwala, Shane Cray

The superbowl last year, Superbowl 52, was memorable for several reasons. It was Tom Brady's eighth superbowl appearance and resulted in the Philidelphia Eagles receiving their first superbowl victory. In addition, it shattered the record for most combined yardage in a superbowl. The final total was 1,151, breaking the previous record by 200 yards. The ball was punted only once in a game that showcased two very dominant offenses. This superbowl raises a lot of questions. Are NFL teams doing better on offense or was this game an outlier? If NFL teams are doing better on offense, what strategies are they employing? Are they passing more or rushing more? Should we expect to see more offensive yards in playoff games?

In this tutorial, we will be using three different datasets which include QB statistics, Total NFL season game averages, and QB passing yard leaders from 1996 to 2016. Further reading about football data can be done at pro football reference and for the ability to craft more detailed queries.
